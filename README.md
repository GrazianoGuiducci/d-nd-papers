# D-ND Framework — Research Papers

A series of seven papers presenting the **Dual-Non-Dual (D-ND) framework**: a mathematical structure emerging from the iterative map $r \to (r+1)/r$, whose fixed point is the golden ratio $\varphi$ and whose dynamics are governed by the matrix $M = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix}$ with $\text{tr}(M)=1$, $\det(M)=-1$.

The framework bridges number theory, quantum mechanics, information geometry, and cosmology through a single algebraic structure.

## Key Results

- **$\det(M) = -1$**: symplectic area preservation (analogue of $\hbar$)
- **$\varphi^2$ universal rate**: convergence tasso independent of input (CV = 0.0000)
- **Tower $1 \to \varphi^2 \to 3$**: closed algebraic hierarchy (Cayley-Hamilton)
- **Algebraic chain**: $M \to \lambda^2 - \lambda - 1 \to \text{disc}=5 \to Q(\sqrt{5}) \to L(s,\chi_5) \to$ GUE
- **Fractal dipole method**: detects GUE statistics where KS test fails ($\beta = 2.007$, $R^2 = 1.000$ on 100K Riemann zeros)
